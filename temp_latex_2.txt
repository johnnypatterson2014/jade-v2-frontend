\section*{Core Rule}

Einstein summation follows two simple rules:

\begin{itemize}
\item Repeated indices are summed over.
\item Indices that remain appear in the output tensor.
\end{itemize}

Example:

\[
C_{ij} = \sum_k A_{ik} B_{kj}
\]

Einsum pattern:

\[
\texttt{ik,kj -> ij}
\]

---

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}

\midrule

\(
x \cdot y = \sum_k x_k y_k
\)
&
\texttt{k,k ->}
\\[10pt]

Matrix Multiplication &
\(
C_{ij} = \sum_k A_{ik} B_{kj}
\)
&
\texttt{ik,kj -> ij}
\\[10pt]

Gram Matrix (Pairwise Dot Products) &
\(
S_{ij} = \sum_k X_{ik} X_{jk}
\)
&
\texttt{ik,jk -> ij}
\\[10pt]

Attention Scores &
\(
S_{bts} = \sum_d Q_{btd} K_{bsd}
\)
&
\texttt{btd,bsd -> bts}
\\[10pt]

Attention Output &
\(
O_{btd} = \sum_s A_{bts} V_{bsd}
\)
&
\texttt{bts,bsd -> btd}
\\[10pt]

Multi-Head Attention Scores &
\(
S_{bhts} = \sum_k Q_{bhtk} K_{bhsk}
\)
&
\texttt{bhtk,bhsk -> bhts}
\\

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section*{Mental Model}

Most deep learning tensor operations follow the same pattern:

\[
A_{\dots k} \; B_{\dots k} \rightarrow \sum_k
\]

Meaning:

\begin{enumerate}
\item Align tensors along a shared dimension.
\item Multiply elementwise.
\item Sum over the shared index.
\end{enumerate}

This produces a matrix or tensor of pairwise interactions.

---

\section*{Key Insight}

Most neural network layers reduce to computing many dot products:

\begin{itemize}
\item Linear layers $\rightarrow$ matrix multiplication
\item Attention $\rightarrow$ query-key dot products
\item Kernel methods $\rightarrow$ Gram matrices
\item Convolutions $\rightarrow$ local dot products
\end{itemize}

Einstein summation simply makes these interactions explicit.
